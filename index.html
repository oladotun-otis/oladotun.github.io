Groundwater Potential mapping in Coastal areas

Groundwater potential mapping using remote sensing techniques involves the use of satellite images and other geospatial data to identify areas with high potential for groundwater resources. Remote sensing data such as topography, land use, and soil characteristics are analyzed to identify factors that influence groundwater occurrence and movement. The information obtained is used to create maps that show areas with high and low potential for groundwater resources. This approach is useful for water resource planning and management, especially in arid and semi-arid regions where groundwater is a critical source of water supply.
